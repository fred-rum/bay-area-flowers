buttercups

Ranunculus spp.
buttercup family (family Ranunculaceae)

Except for extremely rare plants that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • stem angle, rooting at nodes or not, growing from water/mud or not
  • petal number, length
  • leaf shape, especially submerged leaves
  • receptacle bristly or glabrous

creeping buttercup
Ranunculus repens

  • stems decumbent or creeping, rooting at nodes
  • 5 petals, yellow
  • leaf ternate
  • Foothills, Wilder

lobb’s aquatic buttercup
Ranunculus lobbii

  • stem low from water or mud
  • 5 petals, 4–6 mm long, 2–5 mm wide, white
  • floating leaves deeply 3-parted
  • submerged leaves generally 2–3-dissected

common water-crowfoot
Ranunculus aquatilis

  • stem low from water or mud
  • 5 petals, 4–7 mm long, 1–5 mm wide, white
  • floating leaves not described
  • submerged leaves generally 3–6-dissected

cursed crowfoot
Ranunculus sceleratus

  • stem erect from water or mud
  • 3–5 petals, 2–5 mm long, 1–3 mm wide, yellow
  • very rare: Mt. Diablo

See also rough-fruited buttercup (Ranunculus muricatus) below for another potentially aquatic buttercup.

california buttercup
Ranunculus californicus
california buttercup
Ranunculus californicus
  • stems erect to ascending or decumbent (prostrate), generally not rooting at nodes
  • 9–17 petals, 7–14 mm long, 2–6 mm wide, yellow
  • very common

Difficult buttercups

These buttercups all have ≤ 7 petals and potentially grow erect from land. Jepson distinguishes these buttercups by fruit. If you find any with fruit, consult Jepson. Here I’ve tried to distinguish them by flower and leaf characteristics. They are sorted here by petal length.

straightbeak buttercup
Ranunculus orthorhynchus

  • stem erect to decumbent
  • 5–6 petals, 8–18 mm long, yellow adaxially, yellow or red abaxially depending on subspecies
  • receptacle bristly
  • Pleasanton Ridge, Chabot

sacramento valley buttercup
Ranunculus canus var. canus

  • stem erect to decumbent
  • 5–7 petals, 6–12 mm long, 3–6 mm wide, yellow
  • receptacle glabrous
  • basal and proximal cauline leaves 3-parted, segments 1–3-lobed
  • Mission Peak, Sunol, Mt. Diablo
western buttercup
Ranunculus occidentalis
western buttercup
Ranunculus occidentalis
  • stem erect to decumbent
  • 5–6 petals, 5–10 mm long, 3–6 mm wide, yellow
  • receptacle glabrous
  • basal and proximal cauline leaves 3-parted or 1-ternate, ultimate segments entire or dentate
  • Sierra Azul, Del Valle, Mt. Diablo
rough-fruited buttercup
Ranunculus muricatus
rough-fruited buttercup
Ranunculus muricatus
  • stem erect to prostrate, from ground or water
  • 5 petals, 4–8 mm long, 2–4.5 mm [wide], yellow
  • receptacle bristly
  • common

woodland buttercup
Ranunculus uncinatus

  • stem erect
  • 5 petals, 2–4 mm long, 1–2 mm wide, yellow
  • receptacle glabrous
  • rare

pubescent-fruited buttercup
Ranunculus hebecarpus

  • stem erect
  • 0–5 petals, 1.3–2 mm long, 0.3–0.7 mm wide, yellow
  • receptacle glabrous
