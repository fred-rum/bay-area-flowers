wallflowers

Erysimum spp.
Member of mustard family (family Brassicaceae)

Except for extremely rare plants that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • stem base woody or not
  • leaves linear or wider, or narrowly linear or narrower
  • basal/proximal leaf width, measured exactly
  • petal color

western wallflower
Erysimum capitatum
  • stem base not woody
  • leaves linear or wider, 3–15(30) cm wide
  • flower petals orange to yellow (lavender to ± purple)
  • Ecology: open areas, alpine, deserts, woodland, sandy areas[?], chaparral
  • scattered

sand-loving wallflower
Erysimum ammophilum

  • stem base not woody
  • stem 0.4–9(13) dm
  • leaves linear-oblanceolate, 2–9 mm wide
  • flower petals yellow
  • Ecology: coastal dunes
  • rare on Pacific coast near Butano and Big Basin

santa cruz wallflower
Erysimum teretifolium

  • stem base not woody
  • leaves 0.3–3 mm wide, thread-like to narrowly linear, margin ± rolled under, appearing cylindric, finely toothed
  • Santa Cruz area
