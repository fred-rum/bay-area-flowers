master glossary

3-D shapes (e.g. for the involucre)

All involucre shapes may be flared at the tip or not.

bell-shaped
Rapidly widening at the base, then slowly expanding toward the tip.
cylindric
Significantly longer than wide with parallel sides.
rotate
Saucer-shaped. For a corolla, generally means a fused base with little or no tube and spreading limbs.
urn-shaped
Rapidly widening at the base, then narrowed toward the tip.

Geographic locations

Diablo range
The east bay mountains and foothills from Mt. Diablo through Mt. Hamilton and beyond. This is a portion of the inner coast range which extends further through California.
Santa Cruz mountains
The mountains and hills of the SF peninsula, continuing to the central coast. This is a portion of the outer coast range which extends further through California.
Mt. Tam
Mt. Tamalpais. A mountain in Marin County. Part of the outer coast range, but separate from the Santa Cruz mountains.
outer coastal range
In the bay area, this is the Santa Cruz mountains and the Mt. Tam area.

Miscellaneous terms

abaxial
The surface further from the stem when upright. E.g. the lower surface of a leaf or the outer surface of a petal.
adaxial
The surface closer to the stem when upright. E.g. the upper surface of a leaf or the inner surface of a petal.
annual
Lives for one year or less, generally without forming any woody parts.
ascending
Angled up (but less than erect) or angled in the direction of the main axis (but not nearly parallel to the main axis).
basal
Positioned at the base. Most commonly refers to leaves growing at the base of the stem. Contrast cauline.
biennial
Lives for only two years, generally flowering only in the second year. Non-woody.
canescent
Densely covered with fine silvery white hairs.
caudex
Short stem below ground level.
cauline
Growing along a plant’s stem. Most commonly refers to leaves. Contrast basal.
compound
Generally, composed of multiple parts. Opposite of simple.
  • Leaf: divided into distinct leaflets connected by an axis but no blade material.
  • Umbel: an umbel of umbels
cordate
Shaped like a heart in a deck of cards (with tip outward, lobes at the base).
crenate
With shallow, rounded teeth and usually with acute sinuses. Could also be called “scalloped”.
decurrent
The leaf blade extends onto the stem as a short wing.
deltate
Shaped like the Greek letter Δ; an equilateral triangle with corners rounded or not; attached at base.
dehisce
Splitting.
dentate
With teeth pointing outward. Contrast serrate.
dissect
Cut deeply, but not fully divided.
distal
Growing farther away from the base and closer to the tip of a structure. The structure is often implied; e.g. a proximal leaf grows close to the base of the whole plant. Contrast proximal.
divide
Divided into distinct parts, e.g. a compound leaf.
entire
In one smooth shape; i.e. not toothed, lobed, or compound.
erect
Pointing more or less straight up or parallel to the main axis.
exsert
Protruding more than surrounding structures.
free
Growing separately from equivalent structures at the same level, e.g. sepals in the calyx. Compare fused.
fuse
Equivalent structures at the same level that have grown together (more than just touching). Compare free.
glabrous
Without hair.
glaucous
Covered with the appearance of a whitish powder.
herb
Plant without any woody parts.
lanceolate
Long and somewhat narrow; widest between the middle and the base.
leaflet
A division of a compound leaf (which may itself be further compound).
lobe
An protrusion from a larger body, otherwise undifferentiated from the main body. E.g. a corolla lobe is the free part of a petal.
mucro
An abrupt, short, narrow tip/projection.
nodding
Partially drooping, between pendent and spreading.
obovate
Egg shaped; widest between the middle and the tip.
ovate
Egg shaped; widest between the middle and the base.
palmate
With lobes spreading from a common point.
palmate-pinnate
1st-order palmate and 2nd-order pinnate.
papillate
Has a surface with small protuberances, e.g. like the surface of your tongue.
pendent
Drooping or hanging +- straight down.
perennial
Lives for more than two years. Maybe be woody or non-woody.
perennial herb
Non-woody plant that lives for more than two years.
petiole
The stalk of a leaf.
pinnate
Refers to a two-dimensional branched structure. Typically refers to lobes or leaflets in a leaf. 1-pinnate means that the structure has 1 level of branches arising from a central axis. 2-pinnate means that the branches also have branches, etc.
primary leaf
The first one or two leaves of a sprouting monocot or dicot. These are the most proximal leaves. For dicots, the two leaves are opposite.
proximal
Growing closer to the base and farther from the tip of a structure. The structure is often implied; e.g. a proximal leaf grows close to the base of the whole plant. Contrast distal.
recurved
Gradually curved backward. Contrast reflexed.
reflexed
Abruptly bent somewhat or entirely backward. Opposite of erect or ascending. Contrast recurved.
reniform
Like cordate, but distally rounded instead of pointed, and typically less acute at the base.
sagittate
Shaped like an arrowhead, with basal lobes near parallel to the axis.
salverform
With a slender tube and abruptly spreading limbs (e.g. “T” shaped).
scarious
Membranous.
segment
The smallest division of a compound/dissected leaf.
serrate
With teeth pointing tipward. Contrast dentate.
shrub
Woody plant, shorter than a tree, generally with many branches from the base.
simple
Generally, composed of a single part; undivided; unbranched. Opposite of compound.
  • Leaf: composed of a single part (which may be lobed or dissected), not divided into distinct leaflets separated from each other.
spreading
Nearly horizontal or perpendicular to the main axis.
stellate
With 3+ branches radiating from a common point. (Usually used to describe hair.)
stipe
In the knotweed family, a distal extension of the pedicel separated by a line, bulge, or difference in thickness. In ferns, the frond stalk; equivalent to petiole.
stipule
Extension at the base of the petiole.
strigose
Having stiff, straight, appressed hairs.
subshrub
Similar to a shrub, but with only the proximal stems woody. The distal stems are non-woody (or are at least significantly less woody).
subtend
Growing immediately proximal to the compared structure.
taproot
Primary root growing straight down, tapering at the end, with smaller lateral branches.
terminal
The segment at the end, e.g. the most distal segment of a compound or lobed leaf.
tooth
A small, roughly triangular projection. See serrate and dentate. Compare lobe.
truncate
Ending (or beginning) abruptly, e.g. with no tapering.
twig
The ultimate branch segment of a woody plant.
ultimate
The smallest division of a compound/dissected leaf or the final segments of a branching stem.
waif
A plant that has spread with human assistance but cannot reproduce or spread further on its own; will eventually die out assuming no further assistance.
whorl
A cluster of 3 or more (e.g. flowers) with each whorl generally surrounding an axis and multiple whorls interspersed along the axis.
