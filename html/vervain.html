vervain

Verbena spp.
verbana family (family Verbenaceae)

Except for historical records that I'm ignoring, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

western vervain
Verbena lasiostachys
western vervain
Verbena lasiostachys
  • flower bract 1–4.5 mm, generally ≤ calyx
  • stem generally ascending to erect
  • most cauline leaf blades deeply 1–2-lobed near base, coarsely toothed
  • leaf 4–10 cm, petiole < 2 cm
  • common

purpletop vervain
Verbena bonariensis

  • flower bract 1–4.5 mm, generally ≤ calyx
  • stem generally ascending to erect
  • most cauline leaf blades generally unlobed, coarsely serrate
  • leaf 7–15 cm, generally clasping
  • rare

prostrate vervain
Verbena bracteata

  • flower bract 4–8 mm, 2–4 mm > calyx [longer than the flower, even]
  • stem prostrate to decumbent
  • leaf 1–3 cm, base tapered to ± flat petiole
  • very rare
