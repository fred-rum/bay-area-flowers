"Stem distally branched" is a genus characteristic.

prickly sowthistle
Sonchus asper
prickly sowthistle
Sonchus asper
  • annual
  • stem may be distally branched, but usually not close to the inflorescence[?]
  • proximal clasping lobes of cauline leaves rounded, strongly curved to coiled
  • leaf blades dentate, sometimes ± lobed, teeth and lobes soft-spine-tipped
  • involucre 10–13 mm
  • petal < corolla tube
  • fruit flat, 3-ribbed per side, otherwise smooth

common sow-thistle
Sonchus oleraceus
common sow-thistle
Sonchus oleraceus
  • annual
  • stem distally branched [?], often proximally branched [this part is clear]
  • proximal clasping lobes of cauline leaves acute, not curled or coiled
  • leaf blades nearly all lobed [possibly with small teeth], lobes variable in width [narrow lobes may look like sharp teeth], terminal lobe often widely arrowhead-shaped
  • involucre 10–13 mm
  • petal ± = corolla tube
  • fruit flat, 2–4-ribbed [per side?] and cross-wrinkled

perennial sow thistle
Sonchus arvensis

  • perennial
  • stem distally branched
  • basal leaves 5–15 cm, entire to deeply lobed, short-petioled
  • cauline leaves coarsely lobed and toothed
  • proximal clasping lobes of cauline leaves curled
  • peduncles and phyllaries generally sessile-glandular, rarely tomentose
  • involucre 14–18 mm
  • petal ± = corolla tube
  • fruit ± compressed , 3–4-angled, 2-ribbed between angles
  • very rare

There are no other species of this genus in the bay area.
