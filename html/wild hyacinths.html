wild hyacinths

Dichelostemma spp.
Member of asparagus family (family Asparagaceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Key to brodiaea-like flowers (subfamily Brodiaeoideae)

Except for extremely rare plants that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • staminode color, leaning in or out [technically, it’s a filament appendage, but it looks like a staminode]
  • perianth tube narrowed above ovary or not

The defining difference is the number of stamens, but I can’t count them without taking apart the flower.

 blue dicks
Dichelostemma capitatum
blue dicks
Dichelostemma capitatum
  • perianth blue, blue-purple, pink-purple, or white
  • staminodes erect, leaning toward anthers, outward at tip, ± white
  • perianth tube not narrowed above ovary
  • perianth parts generally wider than ookow
  • bracts generally dark purple (paler, striped dark purple)
  • inflorescence has few–many flowers, but I’ve seen no clusters greater than hemispheric.
  • flowers from February–May
  • common throughout the bay area
 ookow
Dichelostemma congestum
ookow
Dichelostemma congestum
  • perianth blue-purple
  • staminodes erect, leaning away from anthers, ± purple
  • perianth tube narrowed above ovary or ± so
  • perianth parts generally narrower than ookow
  • bracts pale purple to green [not consistently distinct in CalPhotos]
  • inflorescence has few–many flowers, possibly so many that the cluster is near spherical, but that’s rare
  • flowers from May–June [from April, really]
  • scattered throughout the bay area
