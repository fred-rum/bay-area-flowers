fritillaries

Fritillaria spp.
Member of lily family (family Liliaceae)

Except for historical records that I'm ignoring, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

checker lily
Fritillaria affinis
  • leaves < 10 or in whorls of 2–8 on lower stem, not sickle-shaped
  • [all others are have leaves ≤ 10 or alternate (crowded), occasionally sickle-shaped]
  • very common
 fragrant fritillary
Fritillaria liliacea
fragrant fritillary
Fritillaria liliacea
  • nectary an obscure narow band 1/2–2/3 perianth
  • perianth white, striped green
  • uncommon

stinkbells
Fritillaria agrestis

  • nectary a prominent narrow band 2/3 to = perianth
  • perianth green-white or yellow abaxially, purple-brown adaxially
  • flower ill-scented
  • Ano Nuevo, very rare in other bay area locations

chocolate lily
Fritillaria biflora

  • nectary a prominent narrow band 2/3 to = perianth
  • perianth dark brown, green-purple or yellow-green
  • flower ill-scented or not
  • rare in San Mateo County
