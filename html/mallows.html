mallows

Malva spp.
mallow family (family Malvaceae)

Key to mallows and checkermallows

There are no other species of this genus in California.

island mallow
Malva assurgentiflora
island mallow
Malva assurgentiflora
  • shrub
    • all others are herbs
  • petals 25–45 mm

common mallow
Malva sylvestris

  • petals generally 15–30 mm, veins dark
    • all others below have shorter petals
  • sepals 6–8 mm
  • bractlets 3–7 mm long, 2–4 mm wide, free

cheeseweed mallow
Malva parviflora

  • petals 3–5 mm
  • sepals ~3 mm
  • bractlets 1–2 mm long, linear to thread-like

dwarf mallow
Malva neglecta

  • petals generally twice as long as sepals (8–12 mm), veins not dark
  • calyx 4–6 mm
  • bractlets widely linear to narrowly lanceolate, bases free from each other and from calyx
bull mallow
Malva nicaeensis
bull mallow
Malva nicaeensis
  • petals 5–12 mm, veins generally dark
  • sepals 4–6 mm
  • bractlets widely lanceolate to ovate, bases fused to calyx

tree mallow
Malva arborea

  • petals 15–20 mm with 5 dark veins
  • calyx ± 4 mm
  • bractlets widely lanceolate to roundish, bases fused to each other, free parts ± 8 mm long, > calyx

cretan tree-mallow
Malva multiflora

  • petals 10–16 mm, generally with 3 dark veins
  • calyx ± 4 mm
  • bractlets widely lanceolate to roundish, bases fused to each other, < calyx
