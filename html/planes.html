planes

Platanus spp.
Member of plane family (family Platanaceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

There are no other species of this genus in California.

Jepson doesn’t include american sycamore (Platanus occidentalis), so I’m using another reference for distinguishing it.

 western sycamore
Platanus racemosa
western sycamore
Platanus racemosa
  • leaf lobes ≥ ⅓ blade
    • terminal lobe much longer than wide
  • 3–7 fruits in a crooked arrangement along a stalk

london plane
Platanus × hispanica

  • leaf lobes < ⅓ blade
    • terminal lobe generally as long as wide

american sycamore
Platanus occidentalis

  • leaf lobes generally shallower than for western sycamore (Platanus racemosa), but it can vary among leaves on a tree and depending on condition
  • usually only a single fruit per stalk
  • extremely rare in the bay area according to CalFlora
