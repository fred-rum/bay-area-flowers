brodiaeas

Brodiaea spp.

Distinguishing characteristics:

  • Length of peduncle (flower stem).
  • Size of corolla.
  • Angle and shape of staminodes.

Labeled photos of brodiaea staminodes.

harvest brodiaea
  • peduncle (flower stem) 10–50 cm.
  • perianth (flower) 29–47 mm (long? wide?)
  • staminodes (crown surrounding stamens) held away from stamens, flat
  • elevation < 8000 ft.

dwarf brodiaea
  • peduncle (flower stem) 0.5–7 cm.
  • perianth (flower) 15–40 mm (long? wide?)
  • staminodes (crown surrounding stamens) leaning toward or parallel to stamens, tips generally reflexed, margins 1/4 incurved below tip.
  • elevation < 1500 ft.
