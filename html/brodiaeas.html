brodiaeas

Brodiaea spp.
asparagus family (family Asparagaceae)

Key to brodiaea-like flowers (subfamily Brodiaeoideae)

Except for old historical records and extremely rare plants that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • length of peduncle (flower stem)
  • size of perianth
  • angle and shape of staminodes [the crown surrounding the stamens]

Brodiaea elegans
  • scape 10–50 cm.
  • perianth 29–47 mm long
  • staminodes held away from stamens, flat
  • elevation < 8000 ft.

 crown brodiaea
Brodiaea coronaria
crown brodiaea
Brodiaea coronaria
  • the height of harvest brodiaea, but with staminodes similar to dwarf brodiaea
  • scape 5–25 cm; pedicel ≤ 7 cm
  • perianth 23–37.5 mm
  • staminodes leaning toward or parallel to stamens
  • Quicksilver, Edgewood, Mt. Tam, and various off-park locations
dwarf brodiaea
Brodiaea terrestris
dwarf brodiaea
Brodiaea terrestris
  • scape 0.5–7 cm (for local ssp.)
  • perianth 15–40 mm long
  • staminodes leaning toward or parallel to stamens, tips generally reflexed, margins 1/4 incurved below tip.
  • elevation < 1500 ft.

starflower brodiaea
Brodiaea stellaris

  • perianth ≤ 25 mm
    • [all others are ≥ 21.5 mm]
  • staminodes about as wide as the perianth lobes
    • [all others have narrower staminodes]
  • extremely rare: restricted area of Windy Hill
