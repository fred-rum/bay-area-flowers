st john’s worts

Hypericum spp.
st. john’s wort family (family Hypericaceae)

Except for historical records that I'm ignoring, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • herb, subshrub, or shrub
  • petal length
  • leaf shape

bog st. john’s-wort
Hypericum anagalloides

  • herb
  • petals 2--4 mm
    • all others are > 5 mm

goldwire
Hypericum concinnum

  • herb
  • leaf linear to lanceolate, generally folded, tip acute
  • petals 10--15 mm
common st. john’s-wort
Hypericum perforatum
common st. john’s-wort
Hypericum perforatum
  • herb
  • leaf narrowly oblong, not folded, tip obtuse
  • sterile axillary branches generally 2--10 cm
  • petals 8--12 mm
  • the only local member is ssp. perforatum

scouler’s st. john’s wort
Hypericum scouleri

  • herb
  • leaf ovate to elliptic, not folded, tip obtuse
  • sterile axillary branches generally < 2 cm
  • petals 7--12 mm
rose-of-sharon
Hypericum calycinum
rose-of-sharon
Hypericum calycinum
  • shrub
  • stem 1 or few-branched
  • flowers generally 1 per stem
  • petals 25--40 mm

canary island st. john’s wort
Hypericum canariense

  • shrub
  • stem many-branched
  • flowers generally > 1 per branch
  • petals 12--17 mm

Hypericum grandifolium

  • shrub
  • large, leathery leaves
  • petals up to ~22 mm
  • not in Jepson's eFlora
