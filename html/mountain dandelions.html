mountain dandelions

Agoseris spp.
Member of sunflower family (family Asteraceae)

Key to dandelion-like flowers

There are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • stem leafy above base or with no more than tiny leaves
  • petal length (absolute or compared to phyllaries)
  • leaf petiole color
  • growing in sand or not

medium-length petals

coast range agoseris
Agoseris hirsuta
coast range agoseris
Agoseris hirsuta
  • stem generally not leafy or obscurely leafy above base
  • ligule 6–16 mm, much exceeding involucre
  • leaf petiole rarely purple
  • generally 5–7 pairs of leaf lobes
  • uncommon

seaside agoseris
Agoseris apargioides

  • stem generally not leafy or obscurely leafy above base
  • ligules 8–16 mm, much exceeding involucre
  • petiole ± purple
  • leaf is either entire or includes generally 3–5 pairs of leaf lobes
  • coastal dunes and sand hills [all others avoid sand]
  • uncommon

spearleaf agoseris
Agoseris retrorsa

  • stem generally not leafy or obscurely leafy above base
  • ligule 6–15 mm, exceeding (equaling) involucre
  • petiole ± purple
  • generally 7–9 pairs of leaf lobes
  • rare

pale goat-chicory
Agoseris glauca var. glauca

  • stem generally not leafy or obscurely leafy above base
  • ligules 10–18 mm, much exceeding involucre
  • petiole not purple
  • leaves have no lobes (but may have a few weak tooths)
  • very rare and out of range

short petals

bigflower agoseris
Agoseris grandiflora var. grandiflora

  • stem generally not leafy or obscurely leafy above base
  • ligule 3–7 mm, ± equaling involucre
  • petiole generally purple
  • generally 3–5 pairs of leaf lobes
  • very common
  • [I assume it’s named for its height, not its flower head]

annual false dandelion
Agoseris heterophylla var. heterophylla

  • stem often leafy above base
  • ligules 2–6 mm, ± equaling involucre [the phyllaries reach almost to the petal tips]
  • leaf petiole not purple
  • generally 2–3 pairs of leaf lobes
  • common
