oats

Avena spp.
Member of grasses (family Poaceae)

Except for extremely rare plants that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

A stiff awn is springs from near the middle of the lemma.

wild oat
Avena fatua
wild oat
Avena fatua
  • glumes 18–32 mm, 9–11-veined
  • 2–3 florets
  • callus bearded
  • lemma soft-hairy in lower ⅓
  • lemma tip ragged or 2-forked with teeth ≤ 1.5 mm
  • awn of lowest floret 25–40 mm, bent, twisted below bend

slender wild oat
Avena barbata

  • glumes 15–30 mm, 5–9-veined
  • 2–3 florets
  • callus bearded
  • lemma generally densely soft-hairy below awn
  • lemma bristle-tipped with teeth 2–6 mm
  • awns 20–45 mm, bent, twisted below bend

oat
Avena sativa

  • glumes 15–50 mm, 7–9-veined
  • 1–2 florets
  • callus glabrous
  • lemma glabrous or nearly so
  • lemma tip acute to minutely 2-forked
  • awn of upper floret 15–30 mm (or none), straight, slightly twisted
  • awn of lower floret < 15 mm (or none)
