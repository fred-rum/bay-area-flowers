false bindweeds

Calystegia spp.

Key to bindweed family (family Convolvulaceae)

Caution: There may be other species of this genus not yet included in this guide.

Key features:

  • small and narrow bracts up to an inch below the calyx, or snuggled up and wide and +- hiding the calyx
  • stem length, climbing or not
  • leaf shape, tomentose or not especially around sinus

Bracts > 1 mm below calyx, not hiding it, ≤ 5 mm wide or variously toothed or lobed like leaves

Calystegia purpurata
  • plant glabrous
  • stem trailing to strongly climbing, +- not intertwined
  • leaf triangular to reniform, lobes wider than linear, 2(3)-tipped, sinus V-shaped or +- closed
  • peduncle generally >> subtending leaf
  • very common

western morning glory
Calystegia occidentalis ssp. occidentalis
  • plant puberulent to finely tomentose
  • stem decumbent to strongly climbing, generally > 1 m
  • leaf lobes generally indistinct, generally 2-tipped; sinus rounded to +- square or tapered
  • peduncle < to +- > subtending leaf
  • common

Bracts < 1 mm below calyx, ± hiding it, > 4 mm wide, not lobed like leaves

Calystegia subacaulis
  • plant hairs sparse to +- dense, short, spreading to reflexed
  • stem poorly developed, generally +- 2 cm
  • leaf +- triangular-hastate, with small backward-directed lobes or not; margin +- wavy or not
  • common

coast range false bindweed
Calystegia collina ssp. collina
  • plant +- tomentose
  • stem decumbent, not or only weakly climbing, 8--15 cm, <= basal rosette of leaves
  • leaf reniform (basal lobes indistinct) to distinctly lobed; margin generally wavy
  • Mt. Tam, Russian Ridge, Sierra Azul, Calero

sea bindweed
Calystegia soldanella
  • stem decumbent, < 60 cm
  • leaf blade 1--3 cm long, 1.5--2 × wider than long, reniform, +- fleshy
  • corolla deep pink or +- purple
  • lives on or very near the beach
  • scattered along the pacific coast
