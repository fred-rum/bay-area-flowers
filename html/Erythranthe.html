Erythranthe spp.

Key to monkeyflowers (family Phrymaceae)

Except for extremely rare plants that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

corolla yellow

calyx uppermost lobe longest, lowest 2 generally upcurved
corolla lower lip base generally swollen, ± closing mouth

seep monkeyflower
Erythranthe guttata
seep monkeyflower
Erythranthe guttata
  • stems 15–65+ cm
  • plant glabrous to hairy distally
  • corolla tube exserted 3–5 mm beyond calyx
magnificent seep monkeyflower
Erythranthe grandis
magnificent seep monkeyflower
Erythranthe grandis
  • stems 50–120+ cm
  • plant densely glandular-puberulent to coarse-hairy distally
  • corolla tube exserted 10–15 mm beyond calyx
  • very rare

calyx lobes generally ± equal or lowest 2 longest, not upcurved
corolla lower lip base not swollen, not closing mouth

coastal monkeyflower
Erythranthe dentata

  • stolons or rhizomes present
  • leaf blade 15–70 mm
  • herbage non-glandular
  • corolla tube generally > 4 mm wide
  • calyx hairy
  • very rare

muskflower
Erythranthe moschata

  • stolons or rhizomes present
  • leaf blade 20–50 mm
  • herbage glandular
  • corolla tube 2–4 mm wide except wider at mouth
  • calyx glabrous to slimy-hairy

purple-stemmed monkeyflower
Erythranthe floribunda

  • neither stolons nor rhizomes present
  • leaf blade 8–25 mm
  • herbage hairy, generally ± slimy
  • calyx ± hairy

broadtooth monkeyflower
Erythranthe latidens

  • neither stolons nor rhizomes present
  • leaf 4–25 mm, proximal-most ± petioled, in basal rosette
  • calyx tube glabrous to puberulent
  • very rare
scarlet monkeyflower
Erythranthe cardinalis
scarlet monkeyflower
Erythranthe cardinalis
  • corolla red or orange-red

rockjasmine monkeyflower
Erythranthe androsacea

  • corolla pink to purple
  • also check the purple flowers in Diplacus spp.
    • leaf bases clasping
    • 5 corolla limbs, ± equal, each 3–7 mm wide
    • calyx generally minutely puberulent, may be green or red-purple
  • very rare
