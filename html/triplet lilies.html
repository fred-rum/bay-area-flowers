triplet lilies

Triteleia spp.

Key features:

  • perianth color (sepals and petals)
  • stamens attached to perianth at 1 level or alternately at 2 levels
  • stamens equal in length or unequal
  • length of perianth tube compared to lobes (for yellow ones)
  • exact pedicel and perianth lengths (for white ones)

ithuriel's spear
Triteleia laxa
  • purple perianth
  • stamens attached alternately at 2 levels, ± equal

golden brodiaea
  • yellow perianth
  • stamens attached at 1 level, unequal
  • tips of longer filaments with forked, pointed appendages
  • perianth tube < lobes
  • perianth lobes ascending to spreading

cook's triteleia
Triteleia ixioides ssp. cookii
  • yellow perianth
  • stamens attached at 1 level, unequal
  • tips of longer filaments with forked, pointed appendages
  • perianth tube ± = lobes
  • perianth lobes reflexed
  • range is south of Big Sur

CalPhotos
coast range triteleia
Triteleia lugens
  • yellow perianth
  • stamens attached at 1 level, unequal
  • tips of longer filaments rounded, without appendages
  • range is outside the bay area, includes Pinnacles NP

white brodiaea
Triteleia hyacinthina
  • white perianth
  • stamens attached at 1 level, ± equal
  • pedicels 0.5–5 cm
  • perianth 9–16 mm, tube 2–4 mm, shallowly bowl-shaped, lobes 7–12 mm, ascending to spreading, midvein green [or blue?]

CalPhotos
long-rayed brodiaea
Triteleia peduncularis
  • white perianth
  • stamens attached alternately at 2 levels, unequal
  • pedicels 2–18 cm
  • perianth 15–28 mm, tube 7–11 mm, acute at base, lobes 10–16 mm [midvein color not mentioned, but CalPhotos shows some with blue, green, or white]

Triplet lilies all have 6 stamens, as opposed to the 3 stamens on the similar-looking brodiaeas (Brodiaea spp.).
