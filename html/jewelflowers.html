jewelflowers

Streptanthus spp.
mustard family (family Brassicaceae)

Except for extremely rare plants that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

bristly jewelflower
Streptanthus glandulosus
bristly jewelflower
Streptanthus glandulosus
  • stem 15–90 cm
  • bilateral calyx
  • variable colors depending on subspecies
  • scattered

mountain jewelflower
Streptanthus tortuosus

  • very similar to S. glandulosus, but
  • raceme bracted below lowest flower or between lowest two flowers [example)
  • very rare at the unvisited edges of the bay area

brewer’s jewelflower
Streptanthus breweri

  • very similar to S. glandulosus, but
  • petals not crinkled
  • on the east side of Mt. Hamilton

tamalpais jewelflower
Streptanthus batrachopus

  • stem 3–17 cm
  • radial calyx, ± red-purple
  • upper 2 petals ± white with purple veins
  • lower 2 petals ± purple
  • Marin

mount hamilton jewelflower
Streptanthus callistus

  • stem 2–9 cm
  • inflorescence includes terminal sterile flower cluster [looks like mangled flowers]
  • bell-shaped calyx, green to ± purple
  • purple petals with darker veins
  • Mt. Hamilton vicinity

mount diablo jewelflower
Streptanthus hispidus

  • stem 3–30 cm
  • inflorescence includes terminal sterile flower cluster [looks like buds?]
  • bell-shaped calyx
  • Mt. Diablo
