woodsorrels

Oxalis spp.
woodsorrel family (family Oxalidaceae)

Except for extremely rare plants that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

purple or pale flowers

Key features:

  • bract location on peduncle
  • leaf arrangement
  • stem branched or none
redwood sorrel
Oxalis oregana
redwood sorrel
Oxalis oregana
  • stem ± 0
  • Leaf: < 10, clustered at rhizome tips
  • petiole < 20 cm, hairy
  • leaflets < 4 cm, often ± purple abaxially, midribs pale
  • Inflorescence: peduncle < 15 cm; bracts 2, near tip
  • sepals < 1 cm, lanceolate
  • petals < 2.5 cm, white to deep pink
  • ± undisturbed forests
pale pink-sorrel
Oxalis incarnata
pale pink-sorrel
Oxalis incarnata
  • Stem: erect, branched, < 30 cm, glabrous; axils often with bulblets
  • Leaf: cauline, in whorl-like clusters of < 10, lower opposite or not
  • petiole 2–7 cm
  • leaflets < 1.5 cm
  • inflorescence: peduncle < 7 cm; bracts 2, near middle
  • sepals < 6 mm, oblong, acute, tips with 2 orange tubercles
  • petals < 2 cm, white to pale pink
  • Edgewood, near Santa Cruz, near Oakland/Berkeley

 purple woodsorrel
Oxalis purpurea
purple woodsorrel
Oxalis purpurea
  • stem ± 0
  • leaf: < 8, in basal rosette; petiole < 5 cm
  • leaflets < 4 cm, sparsely hairy or more densely hairy abaxially and on margins
  • inflorescence: peduncle 1–11 cm; bracts 2, at or below middle
  • sepals < 1 cm, ± lanceolate, hairy
  • petals < 2.5 cm, purple to red, pink, or white [yellow]
  • Nisene Marks, Montara Mtn.

bermuda buttercup

bermuda buttercup
Oxalis pes-caprae
  • stem generally underground
  • leaves in loose, ± basal rosette; petiole < 12 cm
  • leaflets < 3.5 cm, often purple-spotted, abaxially hairy
  • inflorescence: umbel-like, < 20-flowered; peduncle < 30 cm
  • sepals < 7 mm, lanceolate to oblong, tips often with 2 orange tubercles
  • petals < 2.5 cm, yellow

 creeping woodsorrel
Oxalis corniculata
creeping woodsorrel
Oxalis corniculata
  • stem rooting at nodes, < 50 cm, hairs appressed to spreading
  • leaf cauline; petiole < 7 cm
  • leaflets < 2 cm, ciliate, hairs generally 0 adaxially, sparse, appressed abaxially
  • inflorescence: cyme, ± umbel-like or not, 2–7-flowered; pedicel < 1 cm, hairs appressed
  • sepals < 4.5 mm, linear to narrowly ovate
  • petals generally < 8 mm, oblong to spoon-shaped, yellow, sometimes with red spots below middle

 hairy woodsorrel
Oxalis pilosa
hairy woodsorrel
Oxalis pilosa
  • stem erect to decumbent, < 40 cm, rooting at nodes or not, hairs 0.7–1.2 mm, dense, generally spreading
  • leaves cauline
  • leaflets < 1.5 cm, appressed-hairy
  • inflorescence: 1–3-flowered; pedicel < 2 cm, hairs dense, generally spreading, occasionally appressed
  • sepals 3.5–6 mm, lanceolate, tips ciliate
  • petals generally 8–12 mm, yellow

dwarf woodsorrel
Oxalis micrantha
  • stem erect, < 20 cm, hairy
  • leaf cauline; petiole < 5 cm
  • leaflets < 13 mm, hairs sparse, spreading
  • inflorescence raceme-like, 6–14-flowered
  • sepals < 4 mm mm
  • petals < 12 mm, yellow
