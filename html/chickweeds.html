chickweeds

Stellaria spp.
pink family (family Caryophyllaceae)

Except for extremely rare plants that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

Petals are generally entirely divided. If the petals are lobed ≤ 50% of their length, see mouse-ear chickweeds (Cerastium spp.).

common chickweed
Stellaria media
common chickweed
Stellaria media
  • leaves ± evenly spaced
  • internode hairs in a line
  • petals generally present, > 70% sepal length
  • sepals 3–4.5 mm
  • common

lesser chickweed
Stellaria pallida

  • leaves ± evenly spaced
  • internode hairs in a line
  • no petals
  • sepals 2–3 mm
  • very rare

shining chickweed
Stellaria nitens

  • leaves crowded near base
  • no internode hairs or scattered
  • petals < 50% sepal length
  • rare

Rare species outside of my hiking areas are perennial herbs or have sepals > 5 mm.
