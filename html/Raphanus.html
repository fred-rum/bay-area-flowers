Raphanus spp.

mustard family (family Brassicaceae)

There are no other species of this genus in California.

wild radish
Raphanus sativus
wild radish
Raphanus sativus
  • petals pink to purple (or rarely white; check for pale purple veins?)
  • basal leaf blade 2–60 cm long, 1–20 cm wide, entire or pinnately lobed or divided
    • 1–12 lateral lobes, dentate
  • fruit is 2–6 cm long, 5–10 mm wide
    • not constricted between the seeds and does not break up into one-seeded segments when mature

jointed charlock
Raphanus raphanistrum

  • petals pale yellow, ± white in age
  • leaf blade 3–15 cm long, 1–5 cm wide, entire or pinnately lobed
    • 2–8 pairs of lateral lobes (in pairs), dentate
  • fruit is 3–9 cm long, 3–5 mm wide
    • constricted between the seeds and breaks up into one-seeded segments when mature
