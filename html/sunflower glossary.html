sunflower glossary

A single flower head in the sunflower family is actually a tight inflorescence of many flowers, often with separate shapes for the center disk flowers and outer ray flowers.

bristle
A stiff, hairlike tip of a pappus element. [Jepson, master glossary]
disk
The collective term for all disk flowers in a head. [Jepson]
disk flower
A flower in the central portion of a head in the sunflower family. Usually radial and 5-lobed. Often surrounded by ray flowers. [Jepson]
head
A tight inflorescence in the sunflower family which appears from a distance to be a single flower. [Jepson, flower glossary]
involucre
The green base of a flower head in the sunflower family. See also the general definition of involucre. [Jepson, flower glossary]
ligule
A ray-like flower that is clustered only with other ligules to form a head in the sunflower family (i.e. one without disk flowers). [Jepson]
phyllary
A bract of the flower head in the sunflower family. A member of the involucre. [Jepson]
ray
A flower in the outer portion of a head in the sunflower family. Flat and uni-directional, not radial. May be distally lobed. The rays form a ring around the central disk. [Jepson]
scale
Flat, membranous element of a pappus. [Jepson, master glossary, conifer glossary]

Inflorescence shapes

Within the sunflower family, these terms describe the organization of multiple heads in a larger inflorescence. For most of these terms, the order of development among heads is not defined.

cyme
A branched inflorescence in which a head at an axil develops before heads on the subtending branches. [Jepson, flower glossary]
panicle
An inflorescence in which at least some heads are attached to inflorescence branches rather than the central axis. [Jepson, flower glossary, grass glossary]
raceme
An inflorescence with stalked heads attached directly to the inflorescence axis (not to branches). [Jepson, flower glossary, grass glossary]
spike
An inflorescence with sessile heads attached directly to the inflorescence axis (not to branches). [Jepson, flower glossary, grass glossary]
umbel
An inflorescence in which 3+ stalked heads radiate from a common point with no continuation of the central axis. [Jepson, flower glossary, grass glossary]
