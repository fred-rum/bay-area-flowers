tidy tips
Layia platyglossa
  • ray petals are mostly yellow, but usually with white at the tips
  • glandular (e.g. on involucre, or as spots on peduncle)
  • phyllary tip (extending up the petal) ± as long as its folded base
  • stem generally not purple-streaked
  • proximal leaves lobed ± 1/2 to midvein
  • scattered all around the bay

woodland tidytips
Layia gaillardioides

  • most have only yellow; only some have white or pale yellow tips
  • glandular
  • phyllary tip (extending up the petal) much shorter than its folded base [CalPhotos shows at least one exception, but it may be misidentified]
  • stem purple-streaked, ascending, not strictly erect [dunno what this means; they look erect enough on CalPhotos]
  • proximal leaves serrate or lobed “< or > 1/2 to midvein”
  • scattered

smooth tidytips
Layia chrysanthemoides

  • ray petals are mostly yellow, but with white or pale yellow at the tips
  • glandless (e.g. on involucre)
  • stem not purple-streaked
  • proximal leaves often lobed ± to midvein
  • oldish records at Sunol & Edgewood; otherwise mostly historical records

ray petals are always a single color

whitedaisy tidytips
Layia glandulosa

  • only white ray petals
  • glandular
  • Chabot

beach tidytips
Layia carnosa

  • only white ray petals
  • San Francisco, Pt. Reyes

tall tidytips
Layia hieracioides

  • ray petals are short, only yellow
  • scattered, but Windy Hill is the only place I visit regularly
