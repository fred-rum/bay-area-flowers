mule's ears

Wyethia spp.
sunflower family (family Asteraceae)

Key to sunflowers

There are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:
  • phyllary shape; hairy, tomentose, glandular, or glabrous

phyllaries long and wide

smooth mule-ears
Wyethia glabra
smooth mule-ears
Wyethia glabra
  • plant shiny green, glabrous or sparsely short-hairy, glandular
  • outer spreading phyllaries 4–7 cm, wide, leaf-like, generally ≫ inner erect phyllaries
  • phyllaries glabrous or glandular
  • heads generally 1, large
gray mule-ears
Wyethia helenioides
gray mule-ears
Wyethia helenioides
  • plant densely tomentose, often becoming ± glabrous
  • outer spreading phyllaries 4–7+ cm, wide, leaf-like, generally ≫ inner erect phyllaries
  • phyllaries persistently tomentose
  • head generally 1, large

phyllaries short and narrow

narrowleaf mule-ears
Wyethia angustifolia
narrowleaf mule-ears
Wyethia angustifolia
  • phyllaries 1.5–3 cm, narrow, generally not leaf-like, outer ± same as inner
  • phyllaries generally many
  • phyllaries ± soft-hairy, ciliate
  • heads generally 1
Wyethia mollis
  • phyllaries 1.5–3 cm, narrow, generally not leaf-like, outer ± same as inner
  • phyllaries generally few
  • plant ± tomentose, often becoming glabrous
  • heads 1–3
  • very rare
