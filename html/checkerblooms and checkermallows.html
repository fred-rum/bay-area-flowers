checkerblooms and checkermallows

Sidalcea spp.
mallow family (family Malvaceae)

Key to mallows and checkermallows

Except for historical records that I'm ignoring, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

Jepson is fairly useless and doesn’t describe what I see in CalPhotos. Key is based mostly on CalPhotos.

Key features:

  • thin linear or narrowish triangular bracts cupping the calyx or not
  • cauline leaf shape, regularity
dwarf checkermallow
Sidalcea malviflora
dwarf checkermallow
Sidalcea malviflora
  • no bracts cupping the calyx
  • leaf generally dentate or lobed, upper leaves generally much reduced
  • common throughout the bay area
fringed checkerbloom
Sidalcea diploscypha
fringed checkerbloom
Sidalcea diploscypha
  • thin bracts cup the calyx
  • upper leaves have narrow lobes
  • scattered around the bay area
photo
 annual checkerbloom
Sidalcea calycosa
annual checkerbloom
Sidalcea calycosa
  • no bracts cupping the calyx
  • cauline leaves deeply divided into 5–11 narrow lobes [no secondary lobes]
  • Calero & Mt. Tam

marin checkerbloom
Sidalcea hickmanii ssp. viridis

  • bracts cupping the calyx are narrowish triangular, not linear
  • leaves generally all along stem, generally alike in size, shape
  • leaf blade coarsely crenate or shallowly lobed
  • Mt. Tam

maple-leaved checkerbloom
Sidalcea malachroides

  • inflorescence in dense spike
  • leaves generally all along stem, alike in size, shape
  • leaf blade generally shallowly 5–7-lobed
  • Nisene Marks

waxy checkerbloom
Sidalcea glaucescens

  • ID key
  • not found in the bay area
