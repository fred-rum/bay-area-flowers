currants and gooseberries

Ribes spp.
gooseberry family (family Grossulariaceae)

Except for extremely rare plants that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • spines on twig nodes or not
  • bristles between nodes on twigs or not
  • sepal color, spread angle, and number
  • anther color
  • styles hairy at base or glabrous
  • stamen length relative to petals
  • leaf glandular on underside or not

Monterey County Wildflowers appears to be more accurate and useful than Jepson.

fuchsiaflower gooseberry
Ribes speciosum

  • spines at nodes
  • four sepals
    • all others with spines have five sepals
  • red sepals
  • red petals
  • very long red stamens (anthers red or other)

oak gooseberry
Ribes quercetorum

  • spines at nodes
  • five sepals
  • yellow/cream sepals and petals
  • anther filaments not longer than petals
    • all others with spines and five sepals have anther filaments longer than petals
  • rare in Diablo range

straggly gooseberry
Ribes divaricatum var. pubiflorum

  • styles hairy at least at base [figure]
    • all others in this box have styles that are glabrous at base
  • purple to green sepals
  • white petals
  • green to cream anthers
  • internodes bristly or not

bitter gooseberry
Ribes amarum

  • purple-red sepals
  • white petals
  • purple or yellow-green anthers
  • styles glabrous at base
  • leaf blade abaxially glandular
  • no bristles between nodes
  • rare in Diablo range
