Cytisus spp.

Key to brooms

Except for historical records that I'm ignoring, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

 scotch broom
Cytisus scoparius
scotch broom
Cytisus scoparius
  • yellow corolla
  • banner generally > 10–25 mm
  • calyx glabrous
  • fruit flat, glabrous except margin
  • branches generally 5-angled
  • also called scotch broom
  • common everywhere except the southeast bay
8--10-angled branch
 portuguese broom
Cytisus striatus
portuguese broom
Cytisus striatus
  • yellow corolla
  • banner generally > 10–25 mm
  • calyx appressed-hairy
  • fruit ± inflated, densely white-hairy
  • branches generally 8–10-angled
  • common at low elevations

white spanish broom
Cytisus multiflorus

  • white corolla, possibly with dark or purple lines or spots
  • banner < 10 mm
  • leaflets < 10 mm
  • very rare
