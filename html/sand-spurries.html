sand-spurries

Spergularia spp.
pink family (family Caryophyllaceae)

Except for extremely rare plants that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

There are other sand spurries in the bay area, but I’m hesitant to include them when the differences are so inconspicuous.

saltmarsh sand spurry
Spergularia marina

  • annual, delicate
  • 2–5 stamens
    • all others have 6+
sticky sand-spurrey
Spergularia macrotheca
sticky sand-spurrey
Spergularia macrotheca
  • strongly perennial herb, stout
  • lower main stem 0.8–3 mm diam.
  • 0–2+ leaves per axillary cluster
  • calyx lobes 4.5–7 mm
  • styles 0.6–3 mm
  • variants can be distinguished by flower color and style length (measure precisely!)

hairy sandspurry
Spergularia villosa

  • strongly perennial herb, stout
  • calyx lobes 2.5–4 mm
  • styles 0.4–0.6 mm
  • rare
red sand spurrey
Spergularia rubra
red sand spurrey
Spergularia rubra
  • annual, delicate
  • lower main stem 0.3–0.5 mm diam.
  • leaves barely fleshy, 2–4+ leaves per axillary cluster
  • stipules generally 3.5–5 mm, shiny, white, conspicuous

boccone’s sand spurry
Spergularia bocconi

  • annual, ± delicate
  • leaves ± fleshy, 0–2 per axillary cluster
  • stipules generally 1.5–4.5 mm, dull, white to tan, generally inconspicuous
  • rare

greater sea-spurry
Spergularia media var. media

  • annual, stout (lower main stem generally 1–3 mm diam)
  • leaves clearly fleshy
  • very rare
