manroots

Marah spp.
gourd family (family Cucurbitaceae)

There are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • corolla cup shaped or not, especially when viewed from the side/underneath
california manroot
Marah fabacea
california manroot
Marah fabacea
  • corolla rotate, yellow-green to cream or (especially inland) white
    • [When viewed from beneath, the white part of the corolla is entirely spreading. Only the green part is cupped.]
  • fruit 4–5 cm, ± spheric; prickles sparse to ± dense, < 12 mm, ± stiff, unhooked
  • Ecology: Streamsides, washes, shrubby open areas
coastal manroot
Marah oregana
coastal manroot
Marah oregana
  • corolla generally > 8 mm wide, deeply cup-shaped, white
    • [When viewed from beneath, the cup continues from the green part into the corolla.]
  • fruit 4–8 cm, ± spheric, tapered to beak, generally striped dark green; prickles generally dense, stiff or flexible, generally 0 at tip, unhooked
