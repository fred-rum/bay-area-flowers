eucalyptus

Eucalyptus spp.
myrtle family (family Myrtaceae)

Except for old historical records and extremely rare plants that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:
  • bark smooth, shedding, and light colored; or deeply furrowed and dark colored
  • leaf shape
  • inflorescence shape
  • flowers/fruit fused at base or free
  • hypanthium shape

tasmanian blue gum

tasmanian blue gum
Eucalyptus globulus
  • individual flowers in leaf axils, sessile
    • [the leaf may be not present]
  • fruit glaucous, > 1.5 cm wide
    • all others have fruit not glaucous, < 1 cm wide
  • very common
silver dollar gum
Eucalyptus polyanthemos
  • inflorescence a panicle of umbles
  • rare

umbel inflorescence

red ironbark
Eucalyptus sideroxylon

  • bark deeply furrowed, dark brown to ± black
    • all below plants have smooth, shedding bark (sometimes rough near base), lighter colored
  • inflorescence a 5–7-flowered umbel
  • rare

manna gum
Eucalyptus viminalis

  • inflorescence a 3-flowered umbel
  • uncommon

white peppermint
Eucalyptus pulchella

  • leaves linear, generally < 0.5 cm wide
  • inflorescence a 9–15-flowered umbel
  • fruit valves not exserted
  • very rare

river redbum
Eucalyptus camaldulensis

  • leaves lanceolate, generally > 1 cm wide
  • inflorescence a 7–11-flowered umbel
  • fruit valves exserted
  • bud cap hemispheric, beaked, ± = hypanthium (figure of bud with cap)
  • rare

forest red gum
Eucalyptus tereticornis

  • leaves lanceolate, generally > 1 cm wide
  • inflorescence a 7–11-flowered umbel
  • fruit valves exserted
  • bud cap horn-shaped to conic, not beaked, ± 2 × hypanthium
  • very rare
