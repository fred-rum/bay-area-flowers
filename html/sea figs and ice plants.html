sea figs and ice plants

Carpobrotus spp.

Flower diameter is the most obvious distinguishing feature.

The chilean sea fig's flower diameter is 3–5 cm. It primarily grows on coastal dunes and bluffs. Jepson says that its petals are rose to magenta, but CalPhotos also shows flowers with yellow petals. Its center contains very many yellow/cream stamens.

The sea fig's flower diameter is 8–10 cm. It has been planted on dunes and freeway embankments. (Wikipedia has more about its use in California.) Its petals start yellow or white, but fade to pink with age, which matches the color range of the chilean sea fig above.

Other ice plants

In the bay area, the slender iceplant is found mostly on the levees around the salt ponds at the south end of the bay.
