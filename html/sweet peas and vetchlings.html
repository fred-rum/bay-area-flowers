sweet peas and vetchlings

Lathyrus spp.

Distinguishing features:

  • number of leaflets per leaf
  • number of flowers in inflorescence
  • wings or angles on stem (for peas)
  • bristle extending on axis beyond flower (for vetchlings)

pacific pea
Lathyrus vestitus
  • generally 8–12 leaflets per leaf
  • 8–15 flowers in inflorescence
  • stem often sharply angled or flanged (angles or flanges < 1 mm wide)

delta tule pea
Lathyrus jepsonii
  • 10–16 leaflets per leaf
  • 6–15 flowers in inflorescence
  • stem climbing, winged (wings ± ≥ 1 mm wide)
  • L. j. var. californicus is a smaller plant which is sometimes hairy.
  • L. j. var. jepsonii can exceed two meters in height and is hairless, a rare variety which grows in the estuary habitat of the Sacramento-San Joaquin River Delta, the origin of the common name delta tule pea.

broad-leaved sweet pea
Lathyrus latifolius
  • 2 leaflets per leaf
  • 4–15 flowers in inflorescence
  • stem with wings often ≥ 2 mm wide
  • corolla 20–25 mm

red vetchling
Lathyrus cicera
  • 2 leaflets per leaf
  • 1 flower in inflorescence
  • corolla 10–15 mm
  • inflorescence axis not extended beyond flower
  • calyx bowl much shorter than calyx lobes

grass pea
Lathyrus sphaericus
  • 2 leaflets per leaf
  • 1 flower in inflorescence
  • corolla 8–14 mm
  • inflorescence axis extended as bristle beyond flower
  • calyx bowl ± ≤ calyx lobes

See also vetches (Vicia spp.).
