sweet peas and vetchlings

Lathyrus spp.
legume family (family Fabaceae)

Key to vetches vs. vetchlings

Except for historical records that I'm ignoring, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • number of flowers in inflorescence
  • number of leaflets per leaf
  • shape of tendril extending from leaf axis
  • width of wings or angles on stem
  • bristle extending on axis beyond flower
  • length of calyx tube vs. lobes

See also vetches (Vicia spp.) and chaparral pea (Pickeringia montana).

many flowers, many leaflets

pacific pea
Lathyrus vestitus
pacific pea
Lathyrus vestitus
  • 8–15 flowers in inflorescence
  • generally 8–12 leaflets per leaf
  • leaf axis ends in branched, coiled tendril
  • stem often sharply angled or flanged, not winged (angles or flanges < 1 mm wide)
  • corolla 14–20 mm, pale lavendar to dark purple or wine red or white, depending on variety
photo
 delta tule pea
Lathyrus jepsonii
delta tule pea
Lathyrus jepsonii
  • 6–15 flowers in inflorescence
  • 10–16 leaflets per leaf
  • leaf axis ends in branched, coiled tendril
  • stem winged (wings ± ≥ 1 mm wide)
  • corolla 15–20 mm, generally pink to pink-purple
  • L. j. var. californicus is a smaller plant which is sometimes hairy.
  • L. j. var. jepsonii can exceed two meters in height and is hairless, a rare variety which grows in the estuary habitat of the Sacramento-San Joaquin River Delta, the origin of the common name delta tule pea.

silky beach pea
Lathyrus littoralis

  • 1–many flowers in inflorescence
  • 0–16 leaflets per leaf
  • leaf axis ends in short bristle (not coiled or branched tendril)
  • stem angled or flanged, not winged
  • corolla 8–30 mm, pink-purple or pale, occasionally white or yellow
  • Ecology: open coastal dunes

many flowers, two leaflets

broad-leaved sweet pea
Lathyrus latifolius
broad-leaved sweet pea
Lathyrus latifolius
  • 4–15 flowers in inflorescence
  • 2 leaflets per leaf
  • stem with wings often ≥ 2 mm wide
  • corolla 20–25 mm
  • calyx tube > lobes

sweet pea
Lathyrus odoratus

  • 2–4 flowers in inflorescence
  • stem with wings < 2 mm wide
  • 2 leaflets, 3–6 cm, ovate to narrowly elliptic
  • corolla 20–30 mm, white or pale pink to purple
  • calyx tube ± = lobes

tangier pea
Lathyrus tingitanus

  • 2–3 flowers in inflorescence
  • stem with wings < 2 mm wide
  • 2 leaflets, 2–6 cm, lanceolate to widely ovate
  • corolla 25–30 mm, salmon to red-purple
  • calyx tube > lobes

one or two flowers

torrey’s pea
Lathyrus torreyi

  • 1–2 flowers in inflorescence
  • 10–15 leaflets per leaf
  • leaf axis ends in bristle or not (not coiled or branched tendril)
  • corolla 8–10 mm, lilac to pale purple-blue
photo
 hairy vetchling
Lathyrus hirsutus
hairy vetchling
Lathyrus hirsutus
  • 1–2 flowers in inflorescence
  • 2 leaflets per leaf
  • inflorescence axis not extended beyond flower
  • corolla 9–14 mm, pink to blue-purple or 2-colored
  • calyx tube ± = lobes

one flower, two leaflets

red vetchling
Lathyrus cicera
red vetchling
Lathyrus cicera
  • 1 flower in inflorescence
  • 2 leaflets per leaf
  • corolla 10–15 mm, red
  • inflorescence axis terminates at flower pedicel
  • calyx tube ≪ lobes
photo
 grass pea

grass pea
Lathyrus sphaericus
  • 1 flower in inflorescence
  • 2 leaflets per leaf
  • corolla 8–14 mm, red
  • inflorescence axis continues as bristle beyond flower pedicel branch point
  • calyx tube ± ≤ lobes
angled pea

angled pea
Lathyrus angulatus
  • 1 flower in inflorescence
  • 2 leaflets per leaf
  • corolla 9–12 mm, lavender or purple
  • inflorescence axis extended as bristle beyond flower
  • calyx tube ± ≤ lobes
