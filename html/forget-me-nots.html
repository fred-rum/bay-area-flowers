forget-me-nots

Myosotis spp.

broadleaf forget-me-not
  • corolla 5–10 mm wide, blue (but pink when young), prominent yellow ring in center.
  • inflorescence alternating below; flowers denser at the tip, possibly resembling a fiddleneck-like spiral.
  • leaves roughly 2–3x wider than corollas, roughly 2–3x longer than wide.

CalPhotos
changing forget-me-not (Myosotis discolor)
  • smaller corolla, often with different colors for each flower.
  • fiddleneck-like inflorescence.
  • Santa Cruz range.

The forget-me-nots above are the only ones that Jepson thinks grow in the bay area. However, CalFlora has a few others observed here:

CalPhotos
bay forget-me-not (Myosotis laxa)
  • smaller corolla, but the inflorescence otherwise looks much like broadleaf forget-me-not.
  • small, narrow leaves.

CalPhotos
wood forget-me-not (Myosotis sylvatica)

Appears in Calflora, but Jepson says that all identifications in California are mistaken.
