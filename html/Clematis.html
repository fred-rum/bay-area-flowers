Clematis spp.

buttercup family (family Ranunculaceae)

There are no other species of this genus in California.

pipestem clematis
Clematis lasiantha
  • leaflets generally 3--5
  • inflorescence generally 1-flowered
  • sepals 10--21 mm, >> stamens, hairy both abaxially and adaxially
  • flower Jan--Jun
  • Ecology: Hillsides, chaparral, open woodland
  • common

western virgin’s bower
Clematis ligusticifolia

  • leaflets 5--15 [but CalPhotos shows many with 3]
  • inflorescence several- to many-flowered
  • sepals 6--10 mm, +- = stamens
  • flower Jun--Sep
  • Ecology: Along streams, wet places
  • scattered

Clematis pauciflora

  • leaflets generally 3--5
  • inflorescence 1--3(12)-flowered
  • sepals 7--12 mm, +- = stamens, hairy abaxially, but glabrous adaxially
  • flower Jan--Jun
  • Ecology: Dry chaparral
  • rare, mostly in and near Muir Woods
