american asters

Symphyotrichum spp.
Member of sunflower family (family Asteraceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Key to white daisies

Except for old historical records and extremely rare plants that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.
Caution: The key to distinguish these species is not complete.

 pacific aster
Symphyotrichum chilense
pacific aster
Symphyotrichum chilense
  • stem hairy distally
  • outer phyllaries obtuse
  • disk flowers: yellow
  • ray flowers: 8–12 mm, violet
  • very common, especially in the Santa Cruz range

douglas’s aster
Symphyotrichum subspicatum

  • stem ± hairy distally
  • outer phyllaries ± acute
  • disk flowers: yellow
  • ray flowers: 8–16 mm, violet
  • very rare

annual saltmarsh aster
Symphyotrichum subulatum

  • stem ± glabrous
  • disk flowers: yellow
  • ray flowers: 1.5–3 mm, ± white to pink
  • rare

Symphyotrichum lanceolatum var. hesperium

  • rare

Symphyotrichum lentum

  • rare
