clarkia

Clarkia spp.

Distinguishing features:

  • corolla shape and color distribution
  • buds pendant or not
  • all 4 sepals staying fused or not
  • ovary 4-grooved or 8-grooved

Unique corolla shapes

elegant clarkia
  • found everywhere

red ribbons
  • scattered throughout the bay area

CalPhotos
fairy fans (Clarkia breweri)
  • Henry Coe, Sierra Azul and vicinity

    Standard corolla shapes, buds erect or reflexed

    winecup clarkia
    • sepals all coming free or staying fused in pairs
    • found everywhere

    ruby chalice clarkia
    • all 4 sepals staying fused
    • ovary and immature fruit 4-grooved
    • petals with +- red spot or zone at base
    • petals 10--30 mm; stigma exserted beyond anthers
    • found everywhere

    Clarkia franciscana

    • all 4 sepals staying fused
    • ovary and immature fruit 4-grooved
    • petals with +- red spot or zone at base
    • petals 5--13 mm; stigma not exserted beyond anthers
    • Redwood Regional Park

    Clarkia amoena ssp. huntiana

    • all 4 sepals staying fused
    • ovary and immature fruit 4-grooved
    • petals with red spot or marks near middle
    • Marin

    Clarkia affinis

  • all 4 sepals staying fused
  • ovary and immature fruit 8-grooved
  • Sunol, Pleaston Ridge, only historically on the peninsula

    • Standard corolla shapes, buds pendent
