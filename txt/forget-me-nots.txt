{sci:Myosotis}
{+broadleaf forget-me-not}
{-
corolla 5&ndash;10 mm wide, blue (but pink when young), prominent yellow ring in center.
inflorescence alternating below; flowers denser at the tip, possibly resembling a fiddleneck-like spiral.
leaves roughly 2&ndash;3x wider than corollas, roughly 2&ndash;3x longer than wide.
-}

{https://calphotos.berkeley.edu/cgi/img_query?enlarge=0000+0000+1115+3393} {changing forget-me-not:Myosotis discolor}
{-
smaller corolla, often with different colors for each flower.
fiddleneck-like inflorescence.
Santa Cruz range.
-}

The forget-me-nots above are the only ones that Jepson thinks grow in
the bay area.  However, CalFlora has a few others observed here:

{https://calphotos.berkeley.edu/cgi/img_query?enlarge=0000+0000+0208+1294} {bay forget-me-not:Myosotis laxa}
{-
smaller corolla, but the inflorescence otherwise looks much like broadleaf forget-me-not.
small, narrow leaves.
-}
