<h2>Rubus spp.</h2>

Distinguishing features:
<ul>
<li>Prickle presence and shape.</li>
<li>Round or 5-angled stem (pentagonal in cross section).</li>
<li>Fruit type and color.</li>
</ul>

Although the leaves have general difference, there is enough variety within a species to make them difficult to use as diagnostic tools.  In addition, some of the characteristics described by Jepson cannot be seen in CalPhotos.

{california blackberry-2.jpg} {child:california blackberry}
<ul>
<li>Plant: prostrate to decumbent</li>
<li>Prickles: <b>weak, slender</b>, straight</li>
<li>Stem: 2&ndash;10 mm diam, <b>not angled</b>, &plusmn;glabrous to hairy, &plusmn;with stalked glands, glaucous</li>
<li>Sepals: hairy, <b>prickly</b>, &plusmn;with stalked glands</li>
<li>Fruit: <b>blackberry-type</b> (not hollow when picked), <b>black</b></li>
</ul>

{himalayan blackberry-2.jpg} {child:himalayan blackberry}
<ul>
<li>Plant: to 3 m, arched to mounded</li>
<li>Prickles: <b>stout, wide-based</b>, straight or curved</li>
<li>Stem: to 20(25) mm diam, <b>5-angled</b>, finely hairy or generally glabrous, not glaucous</li>
<li>Sepals: hairy, nonglandular</li>
<li>Fruit: <b>blackberry-type</b> (not hollow when picked), <b>black</b></li>
</ul>

{thimbleberry-2.jpg} {child:thimbleberry}
<ul>
<li>Plant 0.5&ndash;2.5 m, erect</li>
<li><b>No prickles</b></li>
<li>Stem: to 6 mm diam, <b>not angled</b>, glabrous to finely hairy with stalked glands, not glaucous</li>
<li>Sepals hairy, stalked-glandular</li>
<li>Fruit: <b>raspberry-type</b> (hollow when picked), <b>red</b></li>
</ul>
