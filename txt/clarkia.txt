<h2>Clarkia spp.</h2>

Distinguishing features:
<ul>
<li>corolla shape and color distribution</li>
<li>buds pendant or not</li>
<li>all 4 sepals staying fused or not</li>
<li>ovary 4-grooved or 8-grooved</li>
</ul>

<h3>Standard corolla shapes, buds erect or reflexed</h3>

{four-spot-.jpg} {child:winecup clarkia}
<ul>
<li>sepals all coming free or staying fused in pairs</li>
<li>found everywhere</li>
</ul>

{ruby chalice clarkia-.jpg} {child:ruby chalice clarkia}
<ul>
<li>all 4 sepals staying fused</li>
<li>ovary and immature fruit 4-grooved</li>
<li>petals with +- red spot or zone at base</li>
<li>petals 10--30 mm; stigma exserted beyond anthers</li>
<li>found everywhere</li>
</ul>

{Clarkia franciscana}
<ul>
<li>all 4 sepals staying fused</li>
<li>ovary and immature fruit 4-grooved</li>
<li>petals with +- red spot or zone at base</li>
<li>petals 5--13 mm; stigma not exserted beyond anthers</li>
<li>Redwood Regional Park</li>
</ul>

{Clarkia amoena huntiana}
<ul>
<li>all 4 sepals staying fused</li>
<li>ovary and immature fruit 4-grooved</li>
<li>petals with red spot or marks near middle</li>
<li>Marin</li>
</ul>

{Clarkia affinis}
<li>all 4 sepals staying fused</li>
<li>ovary and immature fruit 8-grooved</li>
<li>Sunol, Pleaston Ridge, only historically on the peninsula</li>
</ul>
