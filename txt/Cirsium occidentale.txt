This species is complex, variable, and not well described.  Assigning a variety may not always be possible.  My notes are based on a combination of Jepson and Montery County Wildflowers.  I've mostly ignored CalPhotos because their identifications are all over the place.

==venus thistle:C. occidentale var. venustum
color: red
. heads generally long-peduncled, occasionally in tight clusters at peduncle ends, well elevated above proximal leaves
. phyllaries are tomentose, but aren't significantly interconnected by cobwebby hairs
. phyllaries ascending, spreading, or reflexed (the angle of the phyllary's curve, not the angle that it sticks out of the head)
. corolla bright red-pink to red (or rarely other random colors, but <b>generally on the redder side of red-purple</b>)
. common everywhere

==cobwebby thistle:C. occidentale var. occidentale
. heads generally long-peduncled, occasionally in tight clusters at peduncle ends, well elevated above proximal leaves
. phyllaries are significantly interconnected by cobwebby hairs
. phyllaries ascending to spreading (not reflexed) (the angle of the phyllary's curve, not the angle that it sticks out of the head)
. corolla lavender to bright purple (or rarely white, but <b>generally on the more purple side of red-purple</b>)
. common, mostly from the north peninsula to Marin

==california thistle:C. occidentale var. californicum
. heads short- to long-peduncled in +- open cluster, well elevated above proximal leaves
. phyllaries are tomentose, but aren't significantly interconnected by cobwebby hairs
. phyllaries ascending, spreading, or reflexed (the angle of the phyllary's curve, not the angle that it sticks out of the head)
. phyllaries occasionally with sticky-resinous ridge
.. never sticky on other varieties
. <b>corolla pink, pale purple, salmon, or occasionally white/cream</b>
. uncommon, mostly around Mt. Diablo

==compact cobwebby thistle:C. occidentale var. compactum
. heads short-peduncled, closely subtended by basal and large cauline leaves
. phyllaries are significantly interconnected by cobwebby hairs
. <b>corolla dark rose-purple</b>
. very rare near San Bruno Mountain
