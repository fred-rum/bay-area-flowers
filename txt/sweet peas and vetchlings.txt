{sci:Lathyrus}
Distinguishing features:
{-
number of leaflets per leaf
number of flowers in inflorescence
wings or angles on stem (for peas)
bristle extending on axis beyond flower (for vetchlings)
-}

{+pacific pea}
{-
generally <b>8--12 leaflets</b> per leaf
<b>8--15 flowers</b> in inflorescence
stem often sharply angled or flanged (angles or flanges < 1 mm wide)
-}

{https://calphotos.berkeley.edu/cgi/img_query?enlarge=0000+0000+1107+1066} {delta tule pea:Lathyrus jepsonii}
{-
<b>10--16 leaflets</b> per leaf
<b>6--15 flowers</b> in inflorescence
stem climbing, <b>winged</b> (wings &plusmn; &ge; 1 mm wide)
L. j. var. californicus is a smaller plant which is sometimes hairy.
L. j. var. jepsonii can exceed two meters in height and is hairless, a rare variety which grows in the estuary habitat of the Sacramento-San Joaquin River Delta, the origin of the common name delta tule pea.
-}

{+broad-leaved sweet pea}
{-
<b>2 leaflets</b> per leaf
<b>4--15 flowers</b> in inflorescence
stem with wings often >= 2 mm wide
corolla 20--25 mm
-}

{+red vetchling}
{-
<b>2 leaflets</b> per leaf
<b>1 flower</b> in inflorescence
corolla 10--15 mm
inflorescence axis <b>not extended beyond flower</b>
calyx bowl much shorter than calyx lobes
-}

{https://calphotos.berkeley.edu/cgi/img_query?enlarge=0000+0000+1108+1963} {grass pea:Lathyrus sphaericus}
{-
<b>2 leaflets</b> per leaf
<b>1 flower</b> in inflorescence
corolla 8--14 mm
inflorescence axis <b>extended as bristle beyond flower</b>
calyx bowl &plusmn; &le; calyx lobes
-}

See also {-vetches}.
