<h2>Malva spp.</h2>

{bull mallow-1.jpg} {child:bull mallow}
<ul>
<li>petals 5&ndash;12 mm; sepals 4&ndash;6 mm; petals &plusmn; > or >> sepals</li>
<li>petal veins generally dark</li>
<li>bractlets wider than linear, bases fused to each other or to sepals</li>
</ul>

dwarf mallow ({Malva neglecta})
<ul>
<li>petals generally twice as long as sepals; sepals 4&ndash;6 mm</li>
<li>petal veins not dark</li>
<li>bractlets widely linear to narrowly lanceolate, bases free from each other and from sepals</li>
</ul>

cheeseweed mallow ({Malva parviflora})
<ul>
<li>petals 3&ndash;5 mm; sepals ~3 mm</li>
<li>bractlets 1&ndash;2 mm long, linear to thread-like</li>
</ul>

common mallow ({Malva sylvestris})
<ul>
<li>petals generally 15&ndash;30 mm; sepals 6&ndash;8 mm</li>
<li>petal veins dark</li>
<li>bractlets 3&ndash;7 mm long, 2&ndash;4 mm wide, free</li>
</ul>
